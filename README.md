# Geopolitical-Shock-Transmission-in-Global-Energy-Markets

Project Overview

This project analyzes how geopolitical disruptions propagate through interconnected global energy systems using multivariate time-series econometric modeling.

The study focuses on the relationship between:

Maritime shipping activity
Oil throughput
LNG throughput
Brent crude oil prices

Using VAR (Vector AutoRegression), the project examines how shocks in one part of the energy system dynamically affect other variables over time.

Objective

To understand:

1. How energy market shocks spread across interconnected systems
2. Which variables act as major shock transmitters
3. How long disruptions persist before stabilizing
4. The dynamic relationship between logistics, energy supply, and oil prices

The project uses the Hormuz Shipping Crisis 2026 — Daily Energy Market Dataset, which captures daily dynamics across maritime logistics, energy throughput, and global crude oil pricing during geopolitical disruption period.

Key Findings

1. Brent crude price shocks strongly influence oil and LNG throughput dynamics.
2. Shipping disruptions generate delayed but persistent effects across energy systems.
3. Energy market shocks peak within 2–4 periods and gradually stabilize over time.
4. Oil, LNG, and maritime logistics exhibit strong systemic interdependence.

Econometric Techniques Used

1. Vector AutoRegression (VAR)
2. Granger Causality
3. Impulse Response Function (IRF)
4. Forecast Error Variance Decomposition (FEVD)
